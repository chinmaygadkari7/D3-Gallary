Species Extinction

Climate change is affecting the environment all over the world. These effects are observable and serious. Glaciers at the polar regions and mountains are shrinking, sea levels are increasing, global temperature is rising with longer and intense heat waves in various regions.

One of the effects of these changes is occurring on plant and animal species on this planet. Due to the climate change, their natural habitat of animals is being destroyed, making them fight for their survival and hence they migrate to another region. These changes are also affecting the plants, altering the natural conditions required for them to grow. According to the latest estimate, there are 8.7 million species on this planet (ScienceDaily, 2011). Among these species, 150-200 goes extinct every 24 hours (Huffington Post) making this a serious issue for biodiversity, which needs attention from the public.
